BIOTIFY

BIOTIFY is a mobile application intended to assist users by guiding them while they are out and about locally in British Columbia. With its map system, camera function, and organizable photo & video library, the service aims to support Vancouver residents who wish to learn more about their surroundings, making nature exploration simple and accessible.

This service was made for my IXD Visual Design 103 course, with the intention to brainstorm and design a mobile application UI that serves users under a certain design domain. The design domain chosen was Nature Exploration. The application has multiple features, acting as a general hub for nature exploration which allows users’ saved information to be well organized and easily accessible all in one singular app.

With British Columbia’s natural beauty being so vast, outdoorsy people can feel overwhelmed by the influx of unlimited possibilities. Such information may be inaccessible, difficult to find, or even inaccurate, which can lead to confusion and lack of cohesion.

LOG IN & SIGN UP

BIOTIFY is intended to be an account-based application, however it can be used by guests, without making an account. Choosing not to make an account allows users to use the same features as those with an account—the only difference being they cannot permanently save their photo & video entries to the Map or Library; it will be temporary, requiring them to save such entries to their device instead. Having an account on the other hand grants users all organization features, allowing them to save their entries to both the Map & Library. By linking their BIOTIFY account with an email, users can easily save their data and information seamlessly.

ONBOARDING

The application has an onboarding feature when the user uses the app for the first time, giving them a brief walkthrough of all the features the service has to offer. Additionally, users are always free to refer back to this screen via the top leftmost button in the home screen.

MAP [HOME SCREEN]

The home screen/map, has multiple organization features, giving users a bird’s-eye perspective map that opens on their current location. Through the search bar, locations can be navigated to, and with the pinpoint filtering button, certain locations on the map can be hidden and viewed.

At the bottom leftmost corner, the Indigenous Peoples territories can be viewed, displaying information regarding their lands. At the bottom rightmost corner, users can create specific pinpoints based on whether or not their camera’s subject is an animal, plant, or landmark, giving users the creative liberty to organize their map as they like.

Interacting with a pinpoint opens a brief pop-up with brief information on the subject—which can be used to jump to a larger description stored in the Library.

CAMERA

Users can take photos or videos using the camera, with the ability to filter between animals, plants, and landmarks to precisely pinpoint their subject. It features basic photo-taking functions commonly seen in other cameras, such as the flash, perspective swap, and photo gallery.

When snapping a photo, users have the ability to edit their photo or video’s focus to ensure the application’s system is accurately detecting the subject.

After the photo has been taken, the system will load brief information about the subject if the photo was successful, prompting users to save the entry to their Library—if the photo was unsuccessful, users will be prompted to retry.

LIBRARY

All the photos & images that have been taken will converge here into categories if the user chooses to save them. Entries can be located in various ways using the search bar, relevance/date filter, or by subject matter. Photos of the same subject will be categorized together for organization.

Clicking on an entry will open up a page, displaying information regarding the subject, notably including the possible Indigenous annotations.

At the top right is a share button—interacting with this will open up the device’s sharing system, prompting users to share the entry, photo, or video with those in their contacts or through other forms of connection.

SETTINGS

All system features can be managed via the settings, giving users control over their privacy, account, data, etc. This is also where users can reach out for help and support regarding the system, or where they can log out of their account.

Research

The main source of information regarding the subject of nature exploration stemmed from public forums such as Reddit, which contained real-person insights. One key resource was r/vancouverhiking, which contained plenty of information from various people regarding their nature related experiences in British Columbia, providing specific information for our intended service.

While doing research, the main purpose of the intended service changed various times. While our team had decided we wanted to research nature exploration for our domain, we shifted between various ideas for what exactly our service would do. Initially, the service was meant for hikers, displaying travel routes and acting as a social forum for hiking in Vancouver, but that later evolved to a plant, animal, and location identifier with a organization system.
